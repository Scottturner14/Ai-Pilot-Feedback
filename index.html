3
Update Planogram Using Assortment
Updates Using Assortments
Manual pain

Updating a planogram after assortment changes is highly manual and iterative. Planners must remove delisted SKUs, add new products, reposition items.

Rebot value
  • Automatically applies assortment changes (add new, remove delisted)
  • Suggests and executes optimal product placement based on attributes
  • Reduces manual effort and iteration time significantly
📌 Navigate to: Cereals 3 Bay Ai Pilot - Cereal Major Review
“As a space planner, I want to review the new assortment and identify which products are new introductions and which are delisted so that I can plan my additions and removals before updating the planogram.”
Rebot is assortment-aware - when you select the Rebot prompt in the assortment panel or select a new assortment (AI Pilot – Cereal 3 Bay), Rebot will automatically prompt you an option to show adds & delisted products, so you're ready to act straight away.
“As a space planner, I want to remove delisted SKUs from the planogram and add new products to a car park shelf so that I can clear discontinued lines and visually validate new products before placing them into position.”
Ask Rebot to handle your delistings and new lines in one go - e.g. 'move delisted products and new products to a car park shelf.' - so you can validate new items visually before placing them on shelf. Delisted products will appear in Red and new products in Green. (when images are switched off)
“As a space planner, I want to add new products into appropriate locations on the planogram using common attributes so that new SKUs are placed alongside similar products following the merchandising strategy.”
Ask Rebot to place your new products - e.g. 'Add new products to the planogram' - and Rebot will use common attributes like brand, size, and flavor to position them alongside similar products automatically. Products are placed based on their calculated assortment units target.

Validate & Fix
Assisted Editing
Manual pain

Resolving validation warnings is repetitive and time-consuming. Planners must manually review each warning, figure out the fix, and apply corrections one by one across multiple planograms.

Rebot value
  • Reorders planograms by warning count so you tackle the worst first
  • Reviews warnings and suggests fixes in natural language
  • Directly applies fixes where possible (e.g. removing out-of-bay components)
📌 Continue in: Cereals 3 Bay Ai Pilot - Cereal Major Review
“As a space planner, I want to reorder planograms based on validation warnings so that I can prioritize which planograms to work on first by sorting the tab order in Planogram Editor.”
Got a long list of planograms to validate? Ask Rebot to reorder your tabs by validation warnings — e.g. 'Sort my planograms by most warnings first' — so you can tackle the biggest issues first.
“As a space planner, I want to review validation warnings, identify errors and recommended fixes, and suggest and apply corrections so that I can iterate quickly until my planogram is fully compliant.”
Ask Rebot to 'review validation warnings' — it will identify errors that must be fixed. Right now, Rebot can action the removal of components outside the bay space directly. For everything else, Rebot will suggest the fix and guide you through applying it yourself.

6
Add & Validate
Assisted Editing
Manual pain

Adding products, adjusting facings, and checking for assortment gaps are manual and disconnected tasks. Planners must rely on guesswork or separate analyzes, repeatedly validate changes, and manually compare planograms to ensure consistency and compliance.

Rebot value
  • Add products to planograms and detect assortment gaps across layouts
  • Recommend and apply facings based on sales and inventory data
  • Highlight missing products and summarize changes for quick, data-driven decisions
📌 Continue in: Cereals 3 Bay Ai Pilot - Cereal Minor Review
“As a space planner, I want to add a product/s to the planogram so that I can make consistent updates in one go, with the option to undo if needed.”
Ask Rebot to add a product - e.g. 'Add product 12377 to the planogram'. Rebot will apply the change based on attribute similarities. Changed your mind? Just undo. Product additions will appear in Green. (when images are switched off) Alt/Option + C will toggle change colors on and off
“As a space planner, I want to update facings of my new products, so that I can quickly adjust space allocation based on performance without editing each product one by one.”
Right-click the new product and select 'Analyze Product' and ask Rebot to recommend and apply facing changes based on sales, days of supply, and replenishment needs. Ask rebot to 'Increase facings to meet DOS target.'
“As a space planner, I want to identify where there are assortment progression gaps across all open planograms so that I can identify where products need to be added to comply with my wedge assortment rules.”
Want to find out where the gaps are? Ask Rebot to provide you with a matrix showing products placed by planogram. E.g. 'show assortment progression matrix' and add any product missing.
